# Gameplay

[As the game is constantly getting patched, the specific values of health initialization, weapon spawn timings, etc. will change, in terms of learning the game, the specifics are not relevant.]

The Duel gamemode in Diabotical consists of two players, where in order to win, a player would have to obtain the "golden frag" or golden elimination, which is available after a certain time frame to the player that is ahead in eliminations, where that time frame reduces by 30 seconds for each elimination difference. Initially, the timeframe starts at twelve minutes. With this golden elimination ruleset, close games between evenly skilled players will usually allot the entire twelve minutes plus golden elimination time, whereas evidently unbalanced games between two players end quickly.

For a player to gain an advantage over the opponent, the player must have an understanding of the fundamental rules of the game, before thinking about abstract strategies such as map control or item routing. In Duel, the rules are not complex. A player has health and armor, weapons that are available in the game, and where those weapons and items appear on the different maps of the game.

# Armor and Health

At the start of the game, each player starts off with 125 health and 0 armor, and can obtain up to 200 points each with a decay on values above 100. A player is eliminated if they lose all of their health, and "respawns" or re-emerges on a random spawn point of the map. If the player is eliminated, the frag difference increases. Armor works by absorbing about two-thirds of the total damage taken on a tick.

For example, if a player is 100hp+100armor, and gets hit with a 75dmg PNCR shot, the player would have 75hp+50armor left after the shot.

Armor is very important in mitigating damage, however there are cases where armor does not make a difference; for example, if a player is 25hp+200armor, if the player gets hit by a PNCR shot dealing 75 damage, then no matter what how many armor points the player has, the player would get eliminated.

# Weapons

In Duel, there are 9 weapons that can be carried by a character, Note that not all nine weapons will appear on certain maps. In Duel, most of the gameplay revolves around three weapons: the PNCR, the rocket launcher, and the shaft.

# PNCR

The PNCR is a instant hit weapon that deals 70 damage that increases by 5 with consequetive hits with a maximum damage cap of 100. As it takes 1.45 seconds to fire another shot, there are many risks in missing a PNCR shot. Because of this punishing interval, the PNCR is best used at long ranges.

# Shaft

The shaft is a instant hit weapon with finite range that deals 6 damage with a fire interval of 50 milliseconds. As the hitbox of this weapon is a 3 pixel by 3 pixel square on the center of the screen, precise tracking of the opponent is crucial. Since opponents move across the field-of-view faster when they are closer to the player, they are harder to track physically, and with the shaft's finite range, the shaft is the ideal mid-range weapon.

# Rocket Launcher

The rocket launcher is a projectile weapon that deals 100 damage with rockets that hit opponents directly, and indirect splash damage that deals 94 damage if close but indirect and 5 damage if 124 units away from the explosion. Its fire interval is .8 seconds, and the speed of the rockets are 1000 units per second. More than 3 times the walking speed of the player characters. The rocket launcher can be used as utility also as the player can jump and fire at the ground to reach high places, or cover far distances quickly. This method is also known as rocket jumping. Since the rocket projectiles are not instantaneous, they can be read and dodged from afar. However, at close ranges, the rocket launcher can deal high damage with ease, and useful for trapping the opponent and controlling zones.

# Maps and Item Pickups

In Duel, weapons spawn 5 seconds after pickup and ammo spawns 25 seconds after pickup. Regarding health and armor, there are variable quantities and variable respawn times.
